  • Pearl River Delta

    The Pearl River Delta region, including Hong Kong, Macau and part of Guangdong province, is considered one of the most economically vibrant areas of the People's Republic of China, as well as one of the most important industrial bases in the world.

  • Shenzhen

    Shenzhen is a major city in Guangdong Province. From the establishment of the first Special Economic Zone of China, it experienced an unparalleled transformation, evolving from a world factory of light consumer goods to one of the major innovation and technology incubators in China. In addition, logistics and finance are two greatly important components of Shenzhen economy.

  • Longgang Low Carbon City

    The future “Low Carbon City” is located in Longgang District of Shenzhen. This new project is structured in 4 zones including Sustainable Urban Park, Life Science Park, Business and Production Center, and Production Base. The final purpose is to transform the existing Liulian community into a sustainable city as well as a regional commercial hub, combining industrial, production, commercial and residential areas based on eco-friendly design.

  • Local
    people

    The China EU Future City will primarily take into consideration the specific needs of the local people and at the same time maximize the local economic benefits. The larger the development of the city is, the more employments will be offered. Moreover, local people will be trained with on-site vocational training, receiving advantages not only for themselves but also for the community and the production industries.

  • International
    people

    The China EU Future City will provide a balanced mix of functions, from living facilities and senior care training centre to leisure facilities and high-tech production base, to boost the new generation IT business attractiveness of the area and to make it a top destination for high-tech companies from abroad. The aim is to attract skilled professional people in order to bring an innovative economy. We are sure that the inflow of skilled workers will increase the ability to build knowledge clusters and increase the access to leading-edge ideas and technologies.

  • Temporary
    people

    The China EU Future City offering many living laboratories for diverse experiences in sustainability will also attract temporary workers, responsible for advanced research projects. Their aim will be sustain both the economic and social development of the territory, but mostly to support the scientific development.
